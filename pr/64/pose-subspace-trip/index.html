Pose Subspace Trip

Capture a few extreme poses to define your control axes, then warp a trippy 3D visualization with your body. Your live pose is projected onto a PCA subspace whose coordinates drive the scene.

How to use
  1. Start Camera and allow access — you'll see yourself with a skeleton overlay.
  2. Strike a distinct pose and click Capture Pose. Capture 3+ poses that are as different as possible (arms up, crouched, leaning left/right…). These define your control axes.
  3. Now move: your live pose is projected onto the captured subspace, and the 6 coordinates drive the visualization in real time. The bars show where you are on each axis.
  4. Switch Visualization between Pose Constellation (your skeleton as glowing 3D points) and Fractal Hyperspace. Hit ⛶ Fullscreen for the full trip.

Tips: Sens. sets how far a pose pushes the scene; Smooth trades snappiness for flow. Try Hands mode for fine control. Before 3 poses are captured it runs an idle animation and reacts loosely to your overall position.
